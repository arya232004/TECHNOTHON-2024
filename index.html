About Technothon 2024 ✨

Welcome to Technothon 2024 - A 24-hours Hackathon exclusively for Polytechnic Students. Building on the success of our inaugural event in 2023, Technothon continues to be the epicenter of innovation for the engineering community across Mumbai.

In 2023, we witnessed remarkable creativity and problem-solving skills during our 24-hour nonstop hackathon. The overwhelming response fueled our commitment to providing an even more exhilarating experience in 2024.

At Technothon, our aim is to provide a platform for students across the city to inculcate invaluable skills in product development. By building projects from scratch and competing with fellow mates, participants identify and hone their innovation and problem-solving skills.

Guidelines 📝
1
Eligibility
All the students pursuing a diploma in any stream of engineering are welcomed to participate in Technothon 2023. They should be a student of a registered polytechnic college in and around Mumbai.
2
Team Formation
All team members must belong to the same college; inter-college teams are not permitted. A team should comprise exactly four members, including the team leader. At least one female member is mandatory on each team.
3
Idea Submission
Once your team has decided on a problem statement, you'll need to submit your innovative solution. This should be done during registration, in the form of an abstract and proposal. You can choose to submit in PDF, PPT, or Video Presentation format. We highly recommend the Video Presentation format as it allows problem setters to better understand your proposed solution. To help with your submission, you can use the provided PPT template. Use this opportunity to showcase your creativity and effectively communicate your solutions. Make your mark in Technothon 2024!
4
Shortlisted?
If your team makes it to the Grand Finale, a 24-hour Hackathon, you'll be expected to bring your proposed solution to life. We encourage participants to aim for at least 65% completion of their proposed solutions during the event, turning concepts into tangible products or prototypes. However, to ensure fair competition, teams are not allowed to have the entire product or prototype ready before the Grand Finale. You can, however, have a basic setup in place to make efficient use of the 24 hours provided for product development. Get ready to bring your innovative solution to life during the Grand Finale and showcase your problem-solving skills!
5
Winning Criteria
Each Problem Statement can result in different outcomes, and the determination lies with the Problem Setter. Here are the possible scenarios:
  • Winner: Awarded to the team with the best solution/product among competitors addressing the same Problem Statement.
  • Joint Winners: If two teams independently develop and present identically similar solutions/products, they both share the victory.
  • No Winners: Declared if the problem setters find that none of the developed products effectively solve the provided problem.
Good luck!
